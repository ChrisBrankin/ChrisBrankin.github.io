The A&E trainee:

Doctors who work in this area are dealing with patients who haven't expected to find themselves in hospital.

Patients may be seriously ill and have arrived in hospital in an ambulance or they may have been able to make their own way to hospital.

This group of doctors work to immediately stabilise seriously ill patients and to call on the appropriate specialists to admit the patient so the treatment the patient needs can begin.

They also treat less seriously ill patients themselves before sending them home.

This doctor has just started her training programme to become and A&E consultant.

It will take 6 years to complete and she will then spend several more years gaining experience.

During her training she will spend time learning about anaesthetics, intensive care, medical emergences, trauma and paediatric emergency medicine.
