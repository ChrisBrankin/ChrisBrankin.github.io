Mixed Practice Vet:

Vets in mixed practice not only work with small companion animals but also farm animals (usually cows and sheep) and horses.  

In addition to seeing companion animals in the surgery and performing all the roles of a small animal vet, they visit farms to undertake animal health testing, disease surveillance, treat sick/ injured animal and perform surgery, such as caesarean sections in the field.

They also work with farmers to put in place preventive health measures to prevent disease, improve animal welfare and reduce antibiotic use.

Mixed practices often provide an out of hours emergency service for their clients, meaning a vet is available 24/7 365 days a year.

They will be adept at the diagnosis and management of common problems across a wide range of species but will refer to specialists when required.
