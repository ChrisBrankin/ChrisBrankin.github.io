Physicians diagnose and treat conditions and illnesses with medication rather than operations.

After their first few years as a junior doctor, they go on to specialise in a particular area of medicine.

Some stay in hospital and become specialist consultants like cardiologists (problems of the heart), psychiatrists (problems with mental health) or radiologists (interpreting medical images like x rays and CAT scans).

Others like to look after a wide range people with a variety of health problems and become General Practitioners (GPs).
