The Gastroenterologist:

Doctors in this branch of medicine diagnose, treat and work to prevent gastrointestinal (stomach and intestines) and hepatological (liver, gallbladder, biliary tree and pancreas) diseases.

Because it is such a large area, doctors tend to specialised in one part of the gut.

They used endoscopes (flexible tubes through which camers and small operating instruments can be threaded) to visualise and in some instances, treat parts of the intestines and organs.

This is often the way that cancers of the bowel and stomach are first diagnosed.

Some gastroenterologists will work with surgeons in liver transplant services.

There are a number of other conditions which are life long and because of that,this group of doctors often have very long standing relationships with their patients.
