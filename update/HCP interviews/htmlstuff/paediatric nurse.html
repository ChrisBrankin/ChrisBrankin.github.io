The Paediatric nurse:

Children’s nurses look after young people from birth to 18yrs. Nursing a child is not just a question of caring for a small adult. Children have very specific health needs and these nurses understand how a healthy child develops towards adulthood and so can minimise the impact of illness. This involves working in closely with the parents or guardians.

Communication is a crucial factor when treating children. Adults can express their feelings but a child can’t communicate in the same way. Paediatric nurses are able to interpret children’s behaviour and reactions. Children's nurses can also spot when a child's health worsens, which can often happen rapidly.

A child’s care takes place in a range of settings including hospitals, day care centres, clinics and in the community in a child's home.

Children's nurses are part of multidisciplinary teams that look after patients.

They are at the centre of teams that include doctors, hospital play staff, healthcare assistants, newborn hearing screeners, psychologists and social workers.
